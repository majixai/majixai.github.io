Subject Areas Overview (Focus: 6-7 Year Olds)

This document outlines the key subject areas for a church school curriculum designed for children aged 6-7. The goal is to provide a holistic education that integrates faith, foundational academic skills, and personal development.

Core Subject Areas:

1. Bible Stories & Teachings

Focus: Introduce key narratives from the Old and New Testaments in an age-appropriate manner. Emphasize God's love, Jesus's life and teachings, and foundational biblical truths.

Content Examples: Creation, Noah's Ark, Abraham, Moses (simplified), David & Goliath, Birth of Jesus, Miracles of Jesus, Parables (e.g., Good Samaritan, Prodigal Son), Jesus's Death and Resurrection.

Activities: Storytelling with visuals (flannelgraph, pictures, puppets), simple dramas/role-playing, memory verses (short and impactful), discussions about what the story teaches us.

2. Christian Values & Ethics

Focus: Help children understand and apply Christian values in their daily lives.

Content Examples: Love, kindness, forgiveness, honesty, sharing, respect, patience, obedience (to God and parents/teachers), gratitude, helping others.

Activities: Discussing real-life scenarios, role-playing ethical dilemmas (simplified), creating "kindness" posters, stories illustrating values, service-oriented mini-projects.

3. Early Literacy (Reading & Writing)

Focus: Develop foundational reading and writing skills, often integrated with Bible themes and Christian values.

Content Examples:

  • Reading: Letter recognition, phonics, sight words, reading simple sentences and short stories (including Bible-based readers if available), comprehension (understanding what was read).
  • Writing: Printing letters and words, writing simple sentences, journaling (drawing and writing a sentence about their day or a Bible story), creative writing (e.g., "If I were with Jesus...").

Activities: Phonics games, interactive read-alouds, shared reading, guided writing, creating class books, writing letters (e.g., to a missionary, a sick friend).

4. Basic Numeracy (Math)

Focus: Introduce fundamental math concepts in a hands-on, engaging way.

Content Examples: Counting (to 100+), number recognition, simple addition and subtraction (within 10 or 20), shapes, patterns, measurement (non-standard units, basic standard units like inches/cm), telling time (to the hour/half-hour), basic money concepts (coins).

Activities: Using manipulatives (counters, blocks), math games, number lines, connecting math to Bible stories (e.g., counting disciples, animals on the ark), real-world problem solving (e.g., sharing snacks equally).

5. Arts & Crafts

Focus: Encourage creativity, self-expression, and fine motor skill development, often linked to lesson themes.

Content Examples: Drawing, painting, coloring, cutting, gluing, simple sculpting (playdough), creating collages, crafts related to Bible stories (e.g., Noah's Ark, Nativity scene), seasonal crafts.

Activities: Process art (focus on the experience) and product art (creating a specific item), using a variety of materials.

6. Music & Movement

Focus: Foster a love for music, learn Christian songs and hymns, and allow for physical expression.

Content Examples: Singing hymns and worship songs for children, learning songs about Bible stories and Christian values, simple rhythm instruments (shakers, tambourines, drums), creative movement and action songs.

Activities: Group singing, learning actions to songs, playing simple instruments, musical games, responding to music through movement.

Supporting Subject Areas:

7. Nature/Creation Studies

Focus: Develop an appreciation for God's creation and basic scientific observation skills.

Content Examples: Seasons, weather, plants, animals, human body (simple), caring for the environment.

Activities: Nature walks, observing and drawing nature, simple experiments (e.g., planting seeds), discussing how God created the world, learning about stewardship of creation.

8. Community & Service

Focus: Understand the concept of church as a community and introduce age-appropriate ways to serve others.

Content Examples: Learning about different roles in the church, how we can help each other, simple service projects.

Activities: Making cards for sick members, collecting items for a local charity (e.g., food bank), helping keep the classroom tidy, discussing ways to be a good friend and helper.

Integration:

  • Wherever possible, themes should be integrated across subject areas. For example, if the Bible story is Noah's Ark, art could be making an ark craft, numeracy could involve counting animal pairs, literacy could be reading a story about Noah, and nature study could be about animals or rain.
  • Flexibility is key; respond to children's interests and adjust plans as needed.
  • Emphasize participation, effort, and enjoyment of learning.
