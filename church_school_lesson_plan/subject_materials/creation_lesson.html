Lesson Plan: The Story of Creation

Topic: God Creates the World (Genesis 1)

Core Bible Story Summary

This lesson focuses on the biblical account of Creation from Genesis, emphasizing God's power, creativity, and love. We explore the seven days of creation, from the forming of light and sky to the creation of animals and, finally, humankind. The central message is that God intentionally made a good world and that we are a special part of His creation.

Overall Learning Objectives

  • Students will be able to name at least three things God created.
  • Students will understand that God created the world out of love.
  • Students will be able to express gratitude for God's creation.

Age-Differentiated Activities & Focus

Class 1: Ages 2-5

Focus: Sensory experience and simple concepts. Repetition of "God made..."

  • Story Time (2-3 yo): Use a picture board book of Creation. Point to bright, simple pictures. Focus on single words: "Light!", "Water!", "Sun!". Keep it under 3 minutes.
  • Story Time (4-5 yo): Tell an interactive story using flannelgraph pieces or large puppets for each day of creation. Have children repeat phrases like "And it was good."
  • Activity: Creation Sensory Bins.
    • Day 1 & 2 (Light/Sky): Cotton balls (clouds) in a blue-dyed water bin.
    • Day 3 (Land/Plants): A bin with dirt, sand, and plastic plants/flowers to "plant."
    • Day 6 (Animals): A bin with plastic zoo animals.
  • Craft (4-5 yo): "My Creation Book." Staple 7 pages together. On each page, children glue a simple item: cotton ball for clouds, yellow circle for the sun, animal cracker for animals, etc.
  • Song: "He's Got the Whole World in His Hands" with hand motions.

Class 2: Ages 5-8

Focus: Story sequencing, deeper understanding, and personal connection.

  • Story Time (5-6 yo): Read from a children's Bible storybook with engaging illustrations. Pause to ask simple recall questions: "What did God make on Day 4?"
  • Story Time (7-8 yo): Read selected verses directly from Genesis 1 (e.g., NIV or NLT). Discuss the meaning of words like "dominion" and what it means to be made in "God's image."
  • Activity: "Seven Days of Creation" Chart.
    • Provide a large poster board divided into 7 sections. Have children work together to draw or glue pictures of what was created on each day.
    • (7-8 yo): Have them write a simple label for each day.
  • Discussion Questions (7-8 yo):
    • What is your favorite thing God created? Why?
    • How can we take care of God's beautiful world?
    • What does it mean that you are "wonderfully made"?
  • Craft (5-8 yo): Creation Diorama. Use a shoebox to create a scene from one of the days of creation (e.g., an ocean scene, a garden scene).

Materials Needed

  • Picture board book, Children's Bible, or Bible (NIV/NLT)
  • Flannelgraph set or puppets
  • Sensory bins, water, blue food coloring, cotton balls, dirt, plastic plants, plastic animals
  • Paper, stapler, glue, pre-cut shapes (yellow circles, etc.), animal crackers
  • Poster board, markers, crayons, glue, magazines for cutting out pictures
  • Shoeboxes, craft supplies (construction paper, pipe cleaners, clay)
