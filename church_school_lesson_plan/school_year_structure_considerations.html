School Year Structure Considerations (6-7 Year Olds Church School)

Planning the structure of the church school year helps in organizing curriculum delivery, special events, and ensuring a balanced approach to learning. This document outlines key considerations.

1. Dividing the School Year

Terms/Semesters: Consider dividing the year into logical terms (e.g., 2 to 4 terms). This helps in theming, planning, and providing natural breaks or transition points.

Example 1 (Three Terms):

  • Fall Term: September - December (Advent/Christmas focus towards the end)
  • Winter/Spring Term: January - April/May (Lent/Easter focus)
  • Summer Term (Optional): June - August (Could be VBS, shorter thematic units, or a break)

Example 2 (Two Semesters):

  • Semester 1: September - January
  • Semester 2: February - June

Weekly Structure: Most church schools operate on a weekly basis (e.g., Sunday mornings). The number of sessions per term will depend on this.

2. Curriculum Pacing and Thematic Units

Thematic Units Overarching Terms: You can have broad themes for each term (e.g., Fall Term: "God's Wonderful Creation & Our Helpers"; Winter/Spring Term: "Learning from Jesus").

Multi-Week Subjects/Themes: Many topics, especially Bible narratives or character studies, benefit from being explored over several weeks. This allows for deeper understanding and varied activities.

Example: A unit on "Moses" might span 3-4 weeks:

  • Week 1: Baby Moses & Call of Moses
  • Week 2: Plagues & Passover (age-appropriate)
  • Week 3: Crossing the Red Sea & Journey in the Wilderness
  • Week 4: Ten Commandments (simplified for 6-7 year olds) & God's Provision

Sub-Topics within Subjects: Within a broader subject like "Christian Values," each week or bi-weekly could focus on a specific sub-topic (e.g., Kindness, Forgiveness, Honesty).

3. Incorporating Holidays & Special Church Events

Major Christian Holidays:

  • Advent & Christmas: Dedicate several weeks leading up to Christmas for Advent themes (Hope, Peace, Joy, Love) and the Nativity story.
  • Lent & Easter: Plan activities and lessons focusing on the meaning of Lent (simplified), Holy Week events (age-appropriate), and the Resurrection.
  • Pentecost: A lesson on the Holy Spirit and the beginning of the Church.

Other Church Events: Be aware of your specific church's calendar (e.g., Missionary Sunday, Church Anniversary, Pastor Appreciation). Integrate these where appropriate by explaining their significance in simple terms or doing a related activity.

Secular Holidays with Christian Connections (Optional):

  • Thanksgiving: Focus on gratitude to God.
  • New Year's: Focus on new beginnings with God.
  • Valentine's Day: Focus on God's love and loving others.

4. Flexibility and Review

Build in Flex Weeks: It's wise to have a few "flexible" weeks in your annual plan. These can be used for:

  • Reviewing material.
  • Catching up if some lessons took longer than expected.
  • Responding to children's spontaneous interests or questions.
  • Special guest speakers or activities.

Regular Review Periods: Besides flex weeks, plan for short review sessions, especially before moving to a significantly new topic or at the end of a unit. The "Midterm Review Ideas" template can be useful here.

5. Start and End of Year

Beginning of the Year:

  • Focus on welcoming children, establishing routines, building community, and introducing the idea of learning together about God.
  • "All About Me" activities with a faith connection (e.g., "God Made Me Special").

End of the Year:

  • Review key learnings from the year.
  • Celebrate accomplishments (use "Final Project Ideas" or have a simple celebration).
  • Prepare children for the next step if applicable (e.g., moving to an older class).

Example Term Outline Snippet

Fall Term (September - December) - Theme: "God's Big Story & Our Place In It"

September:

  • Week 1: Welcome! God Made Me & My Church Family (Creation Day 6 focus, community)
  • Week 2: God's Wonderful Creation (Days 1-5)
  • Week 3: Adam & Eve - God's Good Plan (Obedience, Forgiveness - simplified)
  • Week 4: Noah's Ark - God's Care & Promises

October:

  • Week 1: Father Abraham - A Big Family for God
  • Week 2: Joseph - From Prison to Palace (God's Plan, Forgiveness) - Part 1
  • Week 3: Joseph - Part 2
  • Week 4: Harvest Thanksgiving / Creation Care

November:

  • Week 1: Moses - A Baby in a Basket & A Burning Bush
  • Week 2: Moses - Let My People Go! (Focus on God's power and help)
  • Week 3: Advent 1 - Hope (Prophecies of Jesus)
  • Week 4: Advent 2 - Peace (John the Baptist, Preparing for Jesus)

December:

  • Week 1: Advent 3 - Joy (Angels & Shepherds)
  • Week 2: Advent 4 - Love (Mary & Joseph, Birth of Jesus)
  • Week 3: Christmas Celebration / Pageant Practice
  • Week 4: (If meeting) Christmas Story / Happy Birthday Jesus!

This structure provides a roadmap but should remain flexible to the leading of the Holy Spirit and the specific needs and interests of the children in your class.
