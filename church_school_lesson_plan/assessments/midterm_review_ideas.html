Midterm Review Ideas & Checklist

Purpose: To review key concepts, stories, and values covered in the first half of the term/semester in an engaging and non-stressful way. This is more about reinforcing learning and identifying areas for re-teaching than formal testing.

Note for Mrs. Scott: Adapt these review activities for the specific age sub-groups. For 2-3 year olds, review is very informal, through repetition of songs, simple actions, or pointing to familiar pictures. For 4-5 year olds, activities like story retelling with props or simple matching games are good. For 5-8 year olds, more structured games, Q&A, and creative responses can be used. The checklist below is more geared towards 5-8 year olds but can be simplified.

Possible Review Activities:

  1. Story Retell Carousel:
    • Set up stations for different key Bible stories learned.
    • At each station, have props, pictures, or key words related to the story.
    • Children rotate in small groups and retell the story to each other or to a teacher/helper.
  2. "What We've Learned" Big Book/Mural:
    • As a class, create a large book or mural.
    • Each page/section represents a key theme, Bible story, or value.
    • Children contribute drawings, words, or sentences.
  3. Values Charades or Role-Play:
    • Write down key Christian values (kindness, honesty, forgiveness) on cards.
    • Children pick a card and act out a scenario demonstrating that value. Others guess.
  4. Memory Verse Bee (Friendly):
    • Review key memory verses.
    • Make it a fun, supportive group activity rather than a competitive bee. Children can help each other.
  5. Concept Sort/Matching Games:
    • Example (Bible Knowledge): Match characters to their stories (e.g., Noah -> Ark; David -> Goliath).
    • Example (Values): Sort scenario cards into "Helpful" or "Not Helpful" piles.
    • Example (Literacy/Numeracy): Games reviewing specific phonics sounds, sight words, or math facts covered.
  6. "Ask Me Anything" (Teacher Q&A):
    • Children can write down (or dictate) questions they still have about topics covered.
    • Teacher answers them in a group setting.
  7. Show and Tell (Related to Learning):
    • Children bring something from home or create something that reminds them of a lesson (e.g., a craft they made, a picture they drew, an object from nature).
  8. Songs & Hymns Review:
    • Sing through favorite songs and hymns learned during the term. Discuss their meanings.

Follow-up:

  • Note areas where children need more reinforcement.
  • Plan activities to revisit these topics in the coming weeks.
  • Celebrate what they have learned! Consider a small "Learning Celebration" instead of a formal midterm test.
