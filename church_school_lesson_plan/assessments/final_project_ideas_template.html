Final Project Ideas & Planning Template

Purpose: To provide a culminating activity that allows children to demonstrate their learning and understanding of key themes, stories, and values from the year/term in a creative and engaging way. This should be a celebration of learning.

Target Age Group(s):

Theme (if applicable, e.g., "Living like Jesus," "God's Big Story"): _________________________

Note for Mrs. Scott: These project ideas may need significant adaptation for different age groups. For 2-3 year olds, a "final project" might be a simple group song with actions or contributing to a class collage. For 4-5 year olds, they might create individual parts of a larger display or a very simple group skit. For 5-6 year olds, the ideas below are more applicable with guidance. For 7-8 year olds, encourage more independence, detail, and perhaps some research or written components. The "Learning Demonstrated" for each idea should be adjusted based on age-appropriate expectations.

Possible Final Project Formats:

1. Class Bible Story Re-enactment/Play:

Description: The class works together to choose a favorite Bible story (or a compilation of scenes) to act out.

Roles: Simple speaking parts, narrators, costume helpers, set designers (simple backdrops).

Learning Demonstrated: Understanding of Bible narrative, character roles, cooperation, public speaking (simple).

2. "Our Book of Christian Values" / "Bible Heroes Book":

Description: Each child contributes a page to a class book. Each page could focus on a specific Christian value (e.g., kindness, with a drawing and a sentence) or a Bible hero they learned about.

Learning Demonstrated: Understanding of values/Bible characters, writing, drawing, summarizing information.

3. Creation Mural or Diorama Series:

Description: The class (or small groups) creates a large mural or a series of dioramas depicting the days of Creation, or other key Bible scenes (e.g., Nativity, Noah's Ark).

Learning Demonstrated: Understanding of Creation story/Bible narratives, artistic expression, teamwork.

4. "Service Fair" or "Kindness Project Showcase":

Description: If the class has engaged in small service projects or acts of kindness, they can present what they did and what they learned. This could be through posters, short talks, or showing items they created for others.

Learning Demonstrated: Application of Christian values, empathy, communication skills.

5. Hymn/Song Performance with Artwork:

Description: Children learn and perform a selection of hymns or Christian songs learned throughout the year. They can also create artwork to be displayed during their performance, illustrating the themes of the songs.

Learning Demonstrated: Memorization of songs, understanding of lyrical themes, artistic expression, performance.

6. "What I've Learned About God" Sharing Circle:

Description: A more reflective project where each child prepares to share one or two important things they've learned about God, Jesus, or the Bible during the year. This could be accompanied by a drawing or a symbol they create.

Learning Demonstrated: Personal reflection, ability to articulate faith concepts in simple terms.
