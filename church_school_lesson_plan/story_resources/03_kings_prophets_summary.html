Bible Theme 3: Kings & Prophets

Overall Summary of Kings & Prophets

After God's people, the Israelites, settled in the Promised Land, they eventually asked for a king to rule over them, like other nations. God allowed this, and Saul became the first king. However, the most famous and beloved king was David. When David was just a young shepherd boy, he showed great courage and faith in God by defeating a giant named Goliath with only a sling and a stone! God chose David to be king because David had a heart that loved God very much. King David wrote many beautiful songs to God, which we call Psalms.

Even good kings sometimes made mistakes, and often the people and their kings would forget God and His ways. During these times, God sent special messengers called prophets. Prophets like Elijah, Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Daniel were brave men who spoke God's messages to the kings and the people. Sometimes these messages were warnings to turn back to God and live right. Other times, the prophets brought messages of comfort and hope, reminding people of God's love and His promises for the future. Daniel, for example, showed amazing faith when he was thrown into a den of hungry lions for praying to God, but God protected him and kept him safe!
