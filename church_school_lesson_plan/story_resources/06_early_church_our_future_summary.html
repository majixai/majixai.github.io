Bible Theme 6: The Early Church & Our Future with God

Overall Summary of The Early Church & Our Future

After Jesus rose from the dead and went back to heaven, He didn't leave His followers alone. He sent the Holy Spirit to be with them, to help them, and to give them power to tell others about Jesus. This happened on a special day called Pentecost, and it was like the birthday of the Church! The disciples, filled with the Holy Spirit, started bravely telling everyone in Jerusalem about Jesus â€“ how He died for their sins and rose again.

Many people believed and joined them. These first Christians loved each other very much. They shared everything they had, prayed together, learned together from the apostles (Jesus's main disciples), and helped anyone in need. The Church began to grow and spread. Men like Peter and John performed miracles in Jesus' name. A man named Saul, who at first tried to stop the Christians, met Jesus in an amazing way and became Paul, one of the greatest missionaries ever! Paul traveled to many different cities and countries, telling people about Jesus and starting new churches.

Being a follower of Jesus means being part of God's family, the Church. We learn to love God, love others, share the good news about Jesus, and help each other grow in faith. And the Bible tells us that one day, Jesus will come back again! When He does, He will make everything new and perfect. All who believe in Him will live with Him forever in Heaven, a wonderful place with no sadness, no tears, and no more goodbyes. This is the amazing future God has planned for all His children!
