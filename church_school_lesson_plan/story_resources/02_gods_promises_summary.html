Bible Theme 2: God's Promises (Abraham & Moses)

Overall Summary of God's Promises

Even after Adam and Eve left the garden, God didn't give up on people. He continued to show His love and make amazing promises. One day, God spoke to a man named Abraham. God promised Abraham that he would have a huge family, as many as the stars in the sky, and that through his family, all people on earth would be blessed. Abraham trusted God, even when it was hard to see how these promises would come true.

Many years later, Abraham's family, called the Israelites, became slaves in a land called Egypt. They were very sad and cried out to God for help. God remembered His promises and chose a man named Moses to lead His people out of slavery. God showed His mighty power through Moses with amazing signs and wonders, like parting the Red Sea so the Israelites could walk through on dry land! God rescued them and promised to lead them to a special new land He had for them.

During their journey, God gave Moses important rules, called the Ten Commandments, to help His people live in a way that pleased Him and showed love to God and each other. God always keeps His promises, even when it takes a long time or things seem impossible. He is faithful and wants to lead and guide His people.
