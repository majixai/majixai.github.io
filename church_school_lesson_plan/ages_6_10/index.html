Church School Curriculum — Ages 6–10

9-Month School Year (September – May)

Welcome, Teacher!

This curriculum is designed for children ages 6–10 in a church school setting. It spans nine months (September through May) and covers a full arc of Scripture — from Creation to the Early Church — while developing Christian values, literacy, creative arts, and community service.

🌱 Ages 6–7 activities are simpler, more sensory, story-focused, and craft-heavy.
📖 Ages 8–10 activities involve deeper reading, writing, discussion, and independent projects.

Month 1 — September

God's Amazing Creation & Who We Are
1

Overview: We begin the year with wonder! Students explore the creation story, learn that God made them uniquely and lovingly, and welcome each other into the church family.

Wonder & Awe Stewardship Identity in God Community
Memory Verse: "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth." — Genesis 1:1
🎉 Welcome Back Sunday
📸 Class Photo Day
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Activities Ages 8–10 Activities
Week 1 Creation Days 1–4 (Light, Sky, Land, Stars) Sing "He's Got the Whole World"; color a day-by-day creation wheel; act out light vs. dark with flashlights Read Genesis 1:1–19; create an illustrated timeline of creation; write 2 sentences on what they would create if they were God
Week 2 Creation Days 5–6: Animals & People Animal collage craft; "God Made…" fill-in song; name their favorite animal and why God made it Research one animal and explain its design to the class; read Psalm 8; discuss what "image of God" means
Week 3 Day 7: Rest (Sabbath) & God's Love for Me Make a "God Made Me" mirror craft; draw self-portrait; group prayer thanking God for bodies Read Genesis 2:1–3; journal prompt: "How do I rest and honor God each week?"; design a personal "Sabbath plan"
Week 4 Adam, Eve & The Garden; The Fall Act out Garden story with simple props; discuss good choices vs. bad choices; make a simple garden craft Read Genesis 3; write a short newspaper report ("Breaking News from Eden"); discuss consequences and grace

Craft/Art: Nature walk and leaf rubbings; creation mobile using paper plates & string.  |  Music: "All Things Bright and Beautiful"; "He's Got the Whole World in His Hands"

Month 2 — October

Heroes of Faith: Abraham, Isaac & Joseph
2

Overview: Students discover that God keeps His promises and that ordinary people can trust God even in hard times.

Faith Trust Forgiveness Obedience
Memory Verse: "Trust in the Lord with all your heart." — Proverbs 3:5a
🍂 Harvest Festival / All Saints Sunday
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 God Calls Abraham (Leave Your Home) Pack a pretend "travel bag"; discuss a time you had to trust someone; star-counting activity (God's promise) Read Genesis 12:1–9; map Abraham's journey; write about a time they had to trust God or someone else
Week 2 God's Promise: Abraham & Isaac (Three Visitors) Felt board story; make a "promise star" ornament; discuss: what is a promise? Read Genesis 18:1–15, 21:1–7; compare God's promises to human promises; create a "Promises of God" booklet starter
Week 3 Joseph's Coat & His Brothers' Jealousy Make a colorful "coat of many colors" paper craft; discuss jealousy and how it hurts; role-play Read Genesis 37; write a short story from a brother's perspective; explore the theme of favoritism
Week 4 Joseph in Egypt: God Turns Bad to Good Puppet show retelling; discuss: even when things seem bad, God has a plan; share a "hard thing God helped you with" Read Genesis 45:1–15; discuss forgiveness deeply; write a letter Joseph might send to his brothers; discuss Romans 8:28

Craft: Joseph's coat paper weaving; star-count canvas.  |  Music: "Father Abraham"; "Trust and Obey"

Month 3 — November

God's Rescue Plan: Moses & The Exodus
3

Overview: God hears the cries of His people and sends a rescuer. Students learn about courage, God's power, and gratitude — timed beautifully with Thanksgiving.

Courage Gratitude God's Power Justice
Memory Verse: "Give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; his love endures forever." — Psalm 107:1
🦃 Thanksgiving Service
🕯️ Advent Begins (late Nov)
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 Baby Moses: God Protects His Servant Make a basket boat craft; act out the story; discuss: how does God protect you? Read Exodus 2:1–10; write a journal entry from Miriam's perspective; map Egypt geography
Week 2 The Burning Bush: God's Call to Moses Tissue-paper fire craft; discuss: God calls even shy people; practice saying "Here I am, Lord" Read Exodus 3–4; discuss Moses's excuses vs. God's assurances; write about a time they felt called to do something scary
Week 3 The Plagues & Passover (Simplified) Visual plagues chart; Passover meal elements introduction; discuss protection through faith Read Exodus 12:1–14; create a Passover seder plate diagram with meaning; explore links to Jesus as the Passover Lamb
Week 4 Crossing the Red Sea & Thanksgiving to God Rip-paper Red Sea craft; Thanksgiving prayer chain; discuss things to thank God for Read Exodus 14; write a praise song like Miriam's; create a personal "Thanksgiving List" to God

Craft: Plagues flip book; Red Sea watercolor.  |  Music: "Go Down, Moses"; "Give Thanks"

Month 4 — December

Advent: Waiting for Jesus & The Christmas Story
4

Overview: The four weeks of Advent build anticipation for Jesus's birth. Students explore Hope, Peace, Joy, and Love through prophecy, angels, shepherds, and the manger.

Hope Peace Joy Love
Memory Verse: "For to us a child is born, to us a son is given." — Isaiah 9:6a
🕯️ Advent Candle Lighting
🎄 Christmas Pageant / Nativity Play
🎉 Class Christmas Party
Week Advent Theme / Story Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1
Hope		 Old Testament Prophecies & Simeon's Hope (Luke 2:25–35) Make an Advent wreath; light the Hope candle; discuss: what are you hoping for? Read Isaiah 7:14, 9:6; compare prophecy to fulfillment; write a short prayer of hope for the world
Week 2
Peace		 The Angel Gabriel Visits Mary (Luke 1:26–38) Angel craft (paper plate + coffee filter wings); discuss: Peace means trusting God; sing "Silent Night" Read Luke 1:26–56; read Mary's Magnificat; write their own prayer/poem in response to God's calling
Week 3
Joy		 The Shepherds & Angels (Luke 2:8–20) Shepherd's staff craft; act out the angel announcement; shout "Good news of great joy!" Read Luke 2:8–20; discuss: why did God tell shepherds first?; write what the "good news" means to them personally
Week 4
Love		 Jesus Is Born — The Nativity (Luke 2:1–7; Matthew 2) Build a nativity scene from paper; place baby Jesus and sing "Away in a Manger"; gift-giving discussion Read Luke 2:1–20 + Matthew 2:1–12; write a newspaper front page about the Nativity; discuss gifts we give God

Craft: Nativity scene; Advent calendar countdown.  |  Music: "O Come All Ye Faithful"; "Hark! The Herald Angels Sing"; "Away in a Manger"

Month 5 — January

Jesus Begins His Ministry
5

Overview: A new year! Students meet Jesus as He grows up, gets baptized, calls His disciples, and performs His first miracle. We explore what it means to follow Jesus.

Discipleship Listening to God Courage New Beginnings
Memory Verse: "Come, follow me." — Matthew 4:19a
🎊 New Year Blessings Activity
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 Jesus as a Boy in the Temple (Luke 2:41–52) Draw Jesus as a child; talk about learning at church; discuss: "Did you know I must be about my Father's business?" Read Luke 2:41–52; compare Jesus growing up to their own growth; write a "Growing in Wisdom" goal for the new year
Week 2 Jesus's Baptism & Temptation in the Desert Water/baptism sensory activity; discuss: the Holy Spirit is like a dove; simple craft — dove from paper Read Matthew 3:13–4:11; explore what it means to be "tempted"; create a "Temptation Shield" showing how to resist with Scripture
Week 3 Calling the First Disciples (Peter, Andrew, James, John) Fishing boat craft; game: "Follow the Leader"; discuss: Jesus chose regular people like you Read Matthew 4:18–22; write brief character sketches of the disciples; discuss: what would you leave behind to follow Jesus?
Week 4 Water to Wine: Jesus's First Miracle (John 2:1–11) Simple "water experiment" with food coloring; discuss: Jesus can do amazing things; what would your miracle be? Read John 2:1–11; discuss what this miracle reveals about Jesus's nature; write about a time God did something unexpected

Craft: Disciple name tags; dove mobile.  |  Music: "I Have Decided to Follow Jesus"; "Lord, I Want to Be a Christian"

Month 6 — February

Jesus Teaches Us to Love
6

Overview: February focuses on Jesus's teachings — the Sermon on the Mount, parables of love, and how to pray. Perfectly aligned with Valentine's Day as God's Love Day!

Love Kindness Forgiveness Prayer
Memory Verse: "Love one another as I have loved you." — John 15:12
❤️ Valentine's Day: God's Love Celebration
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 Sermon on the Mount: The Beatitudes (Matthew 5:1–12) Beatitudes illustrated chart; role-play being "peacemakers"; make heart craft with a Beatitude Read Matthew 5:1–12; write a modern paraphrase of each Beatitude; discuss: who in your life lives like this?
Week 2 The Lord's Prayer (Matthew 6:9–13) Teach the Lord's Prayer line by line with actions/motions; make a "prayer rainbow" banner Read Matthew 6:5–13; break down each petition and explain it in their own words; create a personal prayer template
Week 3 The Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25–37) Puppet show; identify helpers in your community; make a "Help Others" bookmark to give away Read Luke 10:25–37; roleplay updated "Good Samaritan" scenarios; write a reflection on being a neighbor in their school
Week 4 The Prodigal Son: God's Forgiving Love (Luke 15:11–32) Act out the story with costumes; talk about "running to meet someone"; create a "Welcome Home" card Read Luke 15:11–32; explore the older brother's reaction; write from the perspective of each character; discuss God's radical forgiveness

Craft: Heart-shaped prayer cards; "Love Your Neighbor" poster.  |  Music: "Jesus Loves Me"; "Love the Lord Your God"; "They'll Know We Are Christians"

Month 7 — March

Lent: Following Jesus to the Cross
7

Overview: Lent is a season of reflection and preparation. Students explore Jesus as healer, His entry into Jerusalem, the Last Supper, and the events of Holy Week in age-appropriate ways.

Sacrifice Humility Compassion Reflection
Memory Verse: "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son." — John 3:16
🌿 Palm Sunday Preparation
✝️ Ash Wednesday / Lenten Activities
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 Jesus Heals: The Blind Man & The Ten Lepers Blindfold trust walk; discuss: how does it feel to be healed?; "Thank You Jesus" card craft Read John 9:1–12; Luke 17:11–19; write a comparison of the grateful leper vs. the nine; discuss healing and gratitude
Week 2 Jesus as Servant: Washing Disciples' Feet (John 13) Simple foot-washing activity (optional); make "Servant's Heart" wristbands; discuss humble service Read John 13:1–17; write a "Service Plan" — three ways to serve others this week; discuss: why did Jesus wash feet?
Week 3 The Last Supper (Matthew 26:17–30) Simple bread & grape juice communion reflection; explain the meaning of sharing; make a "Remember Me" cross craft Read Matthew 26:17–30; discuss the Passover connection; write a reflection on what communion means to them
Week 4 Palm Sunday: Jesus Enters Jerusalem (Mark 11:1–11) Wave palm branches; sing "Hosanna"; make a donkey/palm-branch diorama Read Mark 11:1–11; compare crowd's expectations to Jesus's actual mission; write: "What kind of king did people expect? What kind of king did Jesus become?"

Craft: Cross from palm leaves; purple Lent banner.  |  Music: "Hosanna (Praise Is Rising)"; "Were You There"; "What Wondrous Love Is This"

Month 8 — April

Easter: Jesus Lives! New Life!
8

Overview: The heart of the Christian year! Students explore the crucifixion with sensitivity, celebrate the resurrection with joy, and begin to understand what Jesus's victory means for us.

Resurrection Hope New Life Joy Testimony
Memory Verse: "He is not here; he has risen, just as he said." — Matthew 28:6
🌸 Easter Celebration Sunday
🥚 Easter Egg Hunt
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 Good Friday: Jesus Dies for Us (Age-Appropriate) Torn red paper cross craft; gentle story focus: "Jesus loved us so much He gave everything"; simple prayer of thanks Read Luke 23:26–49; discuss: what was Jesus thinking/feeling?; write a "Thank You" letter to Jesus
Week 2 Easter Sunday: The Empty Tomb! Resurrection Eggs® (12-scene Easter eggs); celebrate with shouts of "He is Risen!"; bright Easter butterfly craft Read Matthew 28:1–10; explore the different appearances of the risen Jesus; write an "eyewitness" news report of the resurrection
Week 3 Jesus Appears to Disciples (Road to Emmaus; Thomas) Walk "the road to Emmaus" as a group activity; discuss: Jesus is always with us; make "Jesus is Here" door hanger Read Luke 24:13–35; John 20:24–29; compare Thomas's doubt to his confession; write about questions they have about faith
Week 4 The Great Commission & Ascension (Matthew 28:18–20) Make a "Go and Tell!" badge; practice saying the memory verse; simple globe craft — "All the World" Read Matthew 28:18–20 + Acts 1:1–11; write a personal "mission statement" as a follower of Jesus; discuss global missions

Craft: Resurrection garden in a pot; butterfly life-cycle craft (new life).  |  Music: "Christ the Lord Is Risen Today"; "Because He Lives"; "Low in the Grave He Lay"

Month 9 — May

The Early Church & Living for God
9

Overview: We close the year by exploring the birth of the Church at Pentecost, key early believers, and what it means to live as God's children in the world today. Finish with a celebration of the year's journey!

The Holy Spirit Community Sharing Faith Service
Memory Verse: "Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; the Lord your God will be with you." — Joshua 1:9
🔥 Pentecost Sunday
🎓 End-of-Year Ceremony & Awards
🌸 Mother's Day Activity
Week Bible Story / Theme Ages 6–7 Ages 8–10
Week 1 Pentecost: The Holy Spirit Comes! (Acts 2:1–21) Red and orange tissue-paper "flame" hats; discuss: the Holy Spirit helps us; wind/fire sensory exploration Read Acts 2:1–41; discuss Peter's sermon; create a "gifts of the Spirit" mind map; compare before/after for the disciples
Week 2 The Early Church: Sharing Everything (Acts 2:42–47) Class sharing activity; make a "Community Jar" — paper strips of how we help each other; sing "They'll Know We Are Christians" Read Acts 2:42–47; compare the early church community to your own church; write: "What would our church look like if we lived like Acts 2?"
Week 3 Paul's Missions & Heroes of the Early Church Map Paul's journeys with stickers; discuss: being brave for God even far from home; simple cross-shaped craft Read Acts 9:1–19 (Paul's conversion); map one of Paul's missionary journeys; write a short biography of a favorite Bible person from the year
Week 4 Year Wrap-Up: My Story of Faith — Who I Am in Christ Create a "Faith Book" (drawing what I learned, my favorite Bible story, my favorite verse); present to class Write a personal faith statement ("I believe…"); create a "Faith Portfolio" highlighting their best work; share three things God taught them this year

Craft: "Fruits of the Spirit" tree; personal "faith book" scrapbook.  |  Music: "Spirit of the Living God"; "This Little Light of Mine"; "Here I Am, Lord"

🧒 Age Differentiation Guide

Ages 6–7

  • Short attention spans — keep activities under 20 min
  • Lots of movement, songs with actions
  • Simple crafts using scissors, glue, crayons
  • Draw pictures + dictate or write 1–2 sentences
  • Repeat stories multiple times for retention
  • Concrete, literal understanding of stories
  • Celebrate participation, not perfection

Ages 8–10

  • Can read Bible passages independently or in groups
  • Capable of journaling, short essays, reflection writing
  • Enjoy debates, discussions, and questions
  • Can work on multi-week projects independently
  • Begin exploring "why" — deeper theology
  • Respond well to real-world application
  • Can take on leadership roles in group activities
