Optional Weekly Prize System Guide (6-7 Year Olds)

This document provides ideas and considerations for implementing an optional weekly random prize winner system for a 6-7 year old church school class. The primary aim should be to add a little fun and positive anticipation, not to create competition or feelings of exclusion.

Core Philosophy

  • Fun & Encouragement: The system should be light-hearted and seen as a bonus, not a primary motivator for good behavior or learning (these should be intrinsic or encouraged through other means).
  • Fairness & Inclusivity: If implementing, ensure the selection process is genuinely random and that, over time, all children have opportunities.
  • Simplicity: Keep the system easy to manage for the teacher.
  • Optionality: This is entirely optional. If it doesn't suit your class or teaching style, feel free to disregard it.

Selection Methods (Random)

  • Names in a Jar/Hat:
    • Write each child's name on a slip of paper.
    • Draw one name at the end of the class on Friday (or your chosen day).
    • Consider if the drawn name is removed for future draws until all have won, or returned to the jar each week. This impacts frequency options below.
  • Numbered Sticks/Objects:
    • Assign each child a number (e.g., based on attendance list).
    • Have numbered popsicle sticks, stones, or tokens. Draw one.
  • "Student of the Week" (modified for randomness):
    • Instead of basing it on merit, you could randomly select a "Star Helper" or "Special Friend" for the week who also gets a small prize at the end of their "term."
  • Digital Random Name Picker:
    • Many free online tools can randomly pick a name from a list you input. (Ensure you have necessary permissions if using student names online).

Frequency Options

Option 1: One Winner Per Week

A single child is chosen each week.

Pros: Simple, quick.

Cons: Some children may wait a long time for their turn, potentially leading to disappointment if not managed carefully.

Management: If using this, it's often best to remove the winner's name from subsequent draws until everyone has had a turn (if the goal is for everyone to win once per semester/year).

Option 2: Enough Winners Per Friday for All in a Term/Year

Each Friday (or chosen day), draw enough names so that every child in the class will have been chosen by the end of a set period (e.g., semester or school year).

Example: 12 students in class, 12 weeks in a semester. Draw one name per week, removing it from the pool. Everyone wins once.

Example: 12 students, wish for everyone to win twice a year (24 prize moments). If ~30 teaching weeks, you might draw one name most weeks, and occasionally two if you need to catch up.

Pros: Ensures fairness and that everyone gets a turn within a predictable timeframe.

Cons: Requires a bit more tracking by the teacher.

Management: Keep a checklist of students and mark off when they have won to ensure everyone is included.

Prize Ideas (Age-Appropriate & Simple)

Prizes should be small, inexpensive, and ideally reinforce positive themes or creativity.

  • Stickers (especially Bible-themed or positive messages)
  • Pencils, erasers with fun designs
  • A special bookmark
  • A small Christian storybook or coloring book (could be from a class library, borrowed for the week)
  • A "Choose a Song" pass for next week's class
  • "Line Leader" or "Teacher's Helper" for a specific activity next class
  • A small craft item (e.g., a few colorful pipe cleaners, a small amount of playdough to take home)
  • A certificate: "Congratulations [Child's Name], you're our Shining Star this week!"
  • Avoid food prizes due to allergies and dietary restrictions unless carefully managed.
  • Consider a "prize box" from which the child can choose one item.
