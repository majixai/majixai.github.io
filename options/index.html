About This Tool

This page provides information and payoff diagrams for various option strategies, categorized by the number of "legs" (individual option contracts) involved. Use the menu on the left to navigate between strategy types.

The payoff diagrams are generated using Plotly.js and show the theoretical profit or loss of the strategy at expiration based on the underlying asset's price. The examples use hypothetical strike prices and premiums.

Disclaimer: This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice. Option trading involves significant risk.

1-Leg Strategies

These are the simplest strategies, involving buying or selling a single call or put option.

Long Call

Outlook: Bullish on the underlying asset.

Construction: Buy one call option.

Max Profit: Unlimited.

Max Loss: Premium paid.

Breakeven: Strike Price + Premium Paid.

Short Put

Outlook: Bullish to Neutral on the underlying asset (expecting it to stay above the strike).

Construction: Sell (write) one put option.

Max Profit: Premium received.

Max Loss: Strike Price - Premium Received (potentially large if stock goes to 0).

Breakeven: Strike Price - Premium Received.

2-Leg Strategies (Spreads)

These strategies involve simultaneously buying and selling options of the same type (calls or puts) or different types on the same underlying asset. They are often used to reduce risk or cost compared to single-leg strategies.

Bull Call Spread (Debit Spread)

Outlook: Moderately Bullish.

Construction: Buy a call with a lower strike, Sell a call with a higher strike (same expiration).

Max Profit: Difference between strikes - Net Premium Paid.

Max Loss: Net Premium Paid.

Breakeven: Lower Strike Price + Net Premium Paid.

Bear Put Spread (Debit Spread)

Outlook: Moderately Bearish.

Construction: Buy a put with a higher strike, Sell a put with a lower strike (same expiration).

Max Profit: Difference between strikes - Net Premium Paid.

Max Loss: Net Premium Paid.

Breakeven: Higher Strike Price - Net Premium Paid.

3-Leg Strategies

These strategies typically involve three different option contracts and often aim to profit from a specific price movement or volatility scenario, often with defined risk.

Call Butterfly Spread

Outlook: Neutral (expecting low volatility and price near the middle strike).

Construction: Buy 1 lower strike call, Sell 2 middle strike calls, Buy 1 higher strike call. Strikes are equidistant.

Max Profit: Middle Strike - Lower Strike - Net Premium Paid (achieved if price = middle strike at expiration).

Max Loss: Net Premium Paid.

Breakeven(s): Lower Strike + Net Premium Paid; Higher Strike - Net Premium Paid.

4-Leg Strategies

Four-leg strategies involve four different option contracts on the same underlying asset. These are often complex and used for specific market outlooks, frequently focusing on volatility or range-bound scenarios with defined risk and reward.

Iron Condor

Outlook: Neutral (expecting low volatility and the price to stay within a defined range).

Construction: Combine a Bear Call Spread (Sell higher strike call, Buy even higher strike call) and a Bull Put Spread (Sell lower strike put, Buy even lower strike put). Typically entered for a net credit.

  • Buy 1 Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Put (Lower Wing)
  • Sell 1 OTM Put (Lower Middle Strike)
  • Sell 1 OTM Call (Upper Middle Strike)
  • Buy 1 OTM Call (Upper Wing)

(All options have the same expiration date. Put strikes are below the current price, Call strikes are above.)

Max Profit: Net Premium Received (achieved if underlying price is between the short put and short call strikes at expiration).

Max Loss: Width of Put Spread - Net Premium Received OR Width of Call Spread - Net Premium Received (whichever spread is wider, usually they are equal) - Net Premium Received. Limited risk.

Breakeven(s): Lower Breakeven = Short Put Strike - Net Premium Received; Upper Breakeven = Short Call Strike + Net Premium Received.

Example: Buy 90P, Sell 95P, Sell 105C, Buy 110C for a net credit.

Iron Butterfly

Outlook: Neutral (expecting very low volatility and the price to pin the middle strike).

Construction: Combine a Short Straddle (Sell At-the-Money Put, Sell At-the-Money Call) with protection (Buy OTM Put, Buy OTM Call). Typically entered for a net credit.

  • Buy 1 Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Put
  • Sell 1 At-the-Money (ATM) Put
  • Sell 1 At-the-Money (ATM) Call
  • Buy 1 Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Call

(All options have the same expiration. The short put and short call share the same middle strike. The long put strike is lower, the long call strike is higher. Wings are typically equidistant from the middle strike.)

Max Profit: Net Premium Received (achieved if underlying price equals the middle strike at expiration).

Max Loss: Width between middle strike and wing strike - Net Premium Received. Limited risk.

Breakeven(s): Lower Breakeven = Middle Strike - Net Premium Received; Upper Breakeven = Middle Strike + Net Premium Received.

Example: Buy 90P, Sell 100P, Sell 100C, Buy 110C for a net credit.

Long Box Spread

Outlook: Arbitrage/Synthetic Loan (Not a directional or volatility play). Aims to lock in a risk-free profit, though usually very small after commissions and bid-ask spreads in efficient markets. Often used for financing purposes.

Construction: Combine a Bull Call Spread and a Bear Put Spread using the same two strike prices.

  • Buy 1 Call (Lower Strike K1)
  • Sell 1 Call (Higher Strike K2)
  • Buy 1 Put (Higher Strike K2)
  • Sell 1 Put (Lower Strike K1)

(All options have the same expiration).

Value at Expiration: Difference between the strike prices (K2 - K1). This is constant regardless of the underlying price.

Profit/Loss: Value at Expiration - Net Debit Paid. If the net debit paid is less than (K2 - K1), there's a theoretical risk-free profit (arbitrage). If the net debit is more, it's a loss.

Note: The payoff diagram is a flat horizontal line at the profit/loss level. While technically 4 legs, its practical use differs significantly from Condors/Butterflies.

Example: Buy 100C, Sell 110C, Buy 110P, Sell 100P. Value at expiration = 10. Profit depends on Net Debit paid.
